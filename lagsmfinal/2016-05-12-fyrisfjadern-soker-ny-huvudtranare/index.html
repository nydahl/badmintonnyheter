Fyrisfjädern söker ny huvudtränare

Annonsen är på engelska då tränaren kan vara av både inhemsk som internationell karraktär

Imagine 2024 to be the Olympic Games where a badminton player from Uppsala takes a medal.
Are you the one who makes this happen? Are you the one who take actions to fulfil our ambitious goals?

Uppsala KFUM Badminton Club Fyrisfjädern is the largest and most successful badminton club in Sweden with ambitious goals in the long and short term. 
To reach our ambitious goals, Fyrisfjädern is now looking to hire a professional head coach to lead this journey. He or she will handle planning, practices and coaching team games and tournaments, as well as being responsible for strategic planning.

Fyrisfjädern facilitates badminton for around 4 000 members, of which 600+ are youths learning to play and dream of badminton. The club has about 150 licensed elite players within the age group of 11-21 as well as senior and veteran players on both national and international level. Our team has won 15 of the last 17 Swedish championships.

Fyrisfjädern’s players frequently attend international tournaments and competitions and aim to be one of the top clubs in Europe. Several of our top players are a part of the Swedish national team and attends Euro Center, hosted in Fyrisfjädern. Hosting Euro Center provides a unique opportunity to work with our players on a daily basis and collaborate with the center’s coach, to achieve the best possible conditions for development.

We look to reinvent and improve ourselves to reach our ambitious goals. We seek a coach who dares to change and explore new methods and techniques to win medals and at the same time inspire all our 4 000 members to do the same.


Key areas of responsibility

Development, training and coaching of elite players
Coaching at Swedish Badminton League
Create a Talent Development Program 
Coaching at our National Sports High School Euro Center collaboration

Requirements

Experience in both individual and team season planning on international top level
3 years high level elite trainer experience 
National team coach experience is a merit 
High level elite player experience is a merit
English or Nordic language speaking
